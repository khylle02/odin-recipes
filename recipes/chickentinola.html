Chicken Tinola

Chicken tinola, or tinolang manok in Tagalog, is translated to chicken stew or soup. While it’s fairly different from chicken adobo, chicken tinola is also a popular comfort food. It’s an indigenous soup from the Philippines and was even included in the Filipino hero Jose Rizal’s novel Noli Me Tángere in the 1800s.

Ingredients

  • 2 lb (907.2 g) chicken wings and drummettes
  • Malunggay / moringa leaves
  • 2 inches of ginger julienned
  • 5 cloves garlic minced

Steps

  1. Pat the chicken dry and put it in a storage container.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, add the vegetable oil and the onions. Saute for about one to two minutes or until the onions are translucent.
  3. Add the chicken into the pot and stir all the ingredients.
  4. Add the water and the rest of the fish sauce into the pot and stir. Raise the heat to medium-high and bring to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat to medium-low and continue a simmer for about 30 minutes covered with the lid.
